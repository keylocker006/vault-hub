Arthur Schlesinger Jr. to President Kennedy, “CIA Reorganization,” Secret, June 10, 1961

John F. Kennedy

30 JUNE 1961

In this June 30, 1961, memorandum to President Kennedy, Arthur Schlesinger Jr. analyzes CIA autonomy and operational shortcomings following the Bay of Pigs. He argues for stronger State Department oversight of clandestine activities, clearer doctrine balancing secrecy with democratic principles, better alignment of operations with foreign policy, and separation of intelligence analysis from covert actions—drawing on the British model. He proposes restructuring to subordinate CIA operations to policy while preserving capabilities.

John F. Kennedy

25 JULY 1962

1962–1963 CIA memoranda record briefings to the President’s Foreign Intelligence Advisory Board on global covert political action programs. Topics include election support (Italy, Brazil, Chile, etc.), labor/cultural fronts (e.g., Congress for Cultural Freedom), Operation Mongoose (Cuba), and operations in Latin America, Southeast Asia, and Europe. JFK Assassination Newspaper Articles, 1963

John F. Kennedy

22 NOVEMBER 1963

BACM Research's "JFK Assassination Newspaper Articles 1963" compiles contemporary U.S. press coverage of President John F. Kennedy's assassination on November 22, 1963, in Dallas.It captures the immediate national shock, eyewitness reports from Dealey Plaza, the swift identification of Lee Harvey Oswald as the suspected lone gunman, his murder by Jack Ruby two days later, and the outpouring of public grief—providing raw, primary-source insights into one of the 20th century’s most pivotal and enduringly debated events.

FBI Vault

Part 1 of 9 to Part 9 of 9

Albert Einstein

This declassified FBI document primarily reproduces a detailed 1932 memorandum from the Woman Patriot Corporation to the U.S. State Department, urging denial of a visa to Albert Einstein. It accuses him of anarchist and communist affiliations, citing his leadership in pacifist groups like the War Resisters' International (which advocated individual resistance to military service and "revolutionary uprisings"), the Anti-Imperialist League, and Workers International Relief. The charges argue Einstein's "militant pacifism," calls for mass refusal of military service, and opposition to organized government violated U.S. immigration laws excluding anarchists, advocates of overthrowing government by force, and those affiliated with such groups. It emphasizes the legal "burden of proof" on the alien and portrays his views as promoting lawlessness and weakening national defense. Subsequent FBI correspondence (1930s–1940s) reflects ongoing inquiries into Einstein's alleged communist sympathies, including from law enforcement and military officials concerned about his potential influence. The file captures early 20th-century suspicions of prominent intellectuals amid rising fears of radicalism.

BIOCHEMICAL RESEMBLANCE BETWEEN ENDOPARSITES AND MALIGNANT TUMORS

CIA

-- OCTOBER 1950

A 1950 Russian article by Prof. V.V. Alpatov (translated and distributed by CIA in Feb 1951) highlights striking biochemical parallels between intestinal endoparasites (e.g., worms) and cancer tumors, supporting older ideas of a possible parasitic nature of malignancy.
